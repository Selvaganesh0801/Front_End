INTERIM CAR SERVICE INTERVALS


An interim service is typically smaller-scale maintnance that occurs between full services, generally evenly six months or 6,000 miles.Interim services often include:


  • Oil change and filter replacement
  • Serpentine belt inspection
  • Wiper blade inspection
  • Tire pressure checks

If you rack up more miles than the average driver,you could benefit from an interim car services


FULL CAR SERVICE INTERVALS


It's generally said that every driver should bring their car in for a full services once a year or every 12,000 miles.A full service typically includes all the checks of an interim service,plus inspecting several other key components,such as:


  • Engine air filter
  • Cabin air filter
  • Coolant
  • Belts and hoses
  • Brake pad

A full service can include more than 50 separte checks on your vehicle to make sure it's running safely and efficiently


MAJOR CAR SERVICE INTERVALS


Major car services are often reserved for the important maintanance items that don't require servicing as often as other parts.Major car services may include all the check from an interim and full service,plus other critical items such as:


  • Brake fluid exchange
  • Spark plug replacement

OIL & FILTER CHANGE


Your car's oil should be changed at intervals ranging from about 3,000-12,000 miles-based on your MSMS.During this service,an expert technician will offer to perform a Courtesy Check and replace your engine oil and filter.


TIRE ROTATION


Tire rotations help spread wear and tear evenly across all four of yoour tires,helping to maximize tread life and keeping traction consistent across each tire's contact patch.A tire rotarion service include removing the tires from their current location and rotating them yo another location on the vehicle.Then new position of your tirea will depend on their tread direcction and your vehicle's drivetrian.


WHEEL ALIGNMENT


Your wheels need to be aligned to ensure your car drivers straight down the road.After a couple thousand miles of road vibration and the occasional pothole,your alignment may need correcting.Wheel alignment include inspection and adjustment to your vehicle's steering and suspension system.

An expert technician will adjust your car's alignment according to OEM specification.And if applicable,they will finish by recalibrating the vehicle's Steering Angel Sensor(SAS) or Advanced Driver Assistance System(ADAD).
